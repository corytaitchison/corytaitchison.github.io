Recombination

The CMB is a relic - its perfect blackbody spectrum is a remnant of an earlier time when the photons were in equilibrium with matter.

The CMB itself is not in equilibrium - the photons we receive have not interacted with the other photons for billlions of years. Instead, it is a relic which has fallen out of thermal equilibrium, but still retains its thermal character.

Prior to recombination, the temperature was high enough that electrons and protons could not bind into neutral atoms - instead, the Universe was a plasma. In this era, matter and photons interact strongly, and were in equilibrium.
