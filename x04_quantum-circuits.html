                                Quantum Simulation

                                A system of qubits typically requires classical complex numbers to keep track of quantum states. Therefore performing quantum simulations on a quantum computer can lead to exponential resource savings.

                                • Each iteration of the quantum algorithm must completely replace the old state with the new one; there is no way to obtain non-trivial information from a intermediate step without significantly changing the algorithm, because the state is quantum
                                • The final measurement must be chosen cleverly to provide the desired result, because it disturbs the quantum state
                                • There are unitary transformations that quantum computers cannot efficiently approximate; therefore, not all Hamiltonians can be efficiently simulated on a quantum computer
