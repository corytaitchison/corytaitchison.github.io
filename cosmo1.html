Overview

The Big Bang Theory is a collection of ideas and calculations that explain what happened in the early Universe, and how it subsequently evolved into the Universe we see around us today.

Cosmological Principle

The Cosmological Principle: On the largest scales, the universe is spatially homogeneous and isotropic.

  • Homogeneous means that it looks identical at every point in space
  • Isotropic means that it looks the same in every direction
  • The Universe is not homogeneous / isotropic in time - only in space
  • This assumption helps simplify trifle details

Evidence

  • CMB has small fluctuations , where the small order of magnitude tells us that the early Universe was extremely smooth
  • Redshift surveys have provided data on hundreds of thousands of galaxies, and sugggests that the distribution of galaxies is roughly homogeneous on distances greater than lightyears
